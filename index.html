    Asynchronous

    This course is designed to be accessible no matter where in the world you’re located. All the lessons, assignments, and worksheets can be done at a time that’s convenient for you. We will have live sessions to dive deeper into certain topics and help you practice your skills, but most learning will occur on your schedule.

    Skills-Based

    Orenda teaches coaches how to work with their clients on developing skills to manage their symptoms. This approach is distinctive from psychotherapy in that there is no engagement with the trauma story. Coaches support their clients in getting stronger, in managing their symptoms, and in working towards becoming their best selves.

    Present-Oriented

    Orenda Coaches focus on the here and now. Survivors will work with their coaches either as an addition to psychotherapy or on its own in effort to build on strengths of the survivor while working towards goals. Orenda coaches work with their clients assuming goodness and capacity in all people while building on the unique characteristics of each client.

    Here’s what you’ll discover inside:


    Module One : Welcome to The Orenda Project!

    In module one we will create a solid foundation for the rest of the course. You’ll discover exactly what trauma is, what you’ll be doing as a trauma coach, and other critical concepts to know before you embark on this journey.

    Module Two : 5 Tenets of Trauma Coaching

    In module two we will discuss the five tenets that underlie Orenda’s approach to working with survivors of trauma. These include Growth after Trauma, Finding Purpose, Authenticity, Self-Agency, Connection & Community.

    Module Three : Application of Principles & Skill Building

    In module three, we will learn an entire menu of skills you can work on with your clients, depending upon the goals they have set for themselves and their current challenges. We will be returning to the tenets of our coaching program here and practice applying them to skill development.

    Module Four : The Nuts & Bolts of Coaching

    In module four, we will discuss The Coaching Relationship from beginning to end. Will will learn how to conduct a coaching intake, how to develop a coaching plan, working in adjunct with psychotherapy, how to develop goals and measure progress.

    Module Five : Ethics of Trauma Coaching

    In module five, we will learn Orenda’s Code of Ethics, which was created to help provide professional guidance in navigating the coaching relationship. Conflicts of interest, cultural humility, privacy, private conduct, self-care, and scope of practice are only some of the areas we will touch upon in this module. Orenda strives to work alongside other coaching trainings to set the standard for ethical coaching.

    What is the difference between coaching and therapy?

    As a coach, it will be up to you to be clear with your clients what differentiates coaching from therapy. As a trauma coach, you will help your clients build the skills they need in order to prepare themselves to address their past trauma, to help support them as they work through it, or to help them work towards being their best selves after recovering. Therapy is a highly contained and controlled environment that allows practitioners who are trained in modalities specifically designed to treat the symptoms of post-traumatic stress to do so safely and successfully. A key feature of therapy is addressing trauma directly, whereas coaches limit their scope to skill building, offering support between therapy sessions, and working towards thriving with trauma in the rear view mirror. Therapy can involve processing the past, while coaching is focused on the present and the future.
